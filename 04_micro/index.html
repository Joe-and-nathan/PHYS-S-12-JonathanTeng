I went in and saw everyone sawing and cutting with all these fancy equipment. I was asked to make something moving, so I decided to make a water pump that relies on the centrifugal force of circular motion. I connected the motor to make the spinning part of the pump which will lead the water to an exit. <div> </div> <div class="col-sm-4"> <img alt="INTRO" src="./ezgif-4-c72dc5e690.gif" class=center> </div> <div> </div> After this, I enclosed the spinning part of the pump inside a plastic pipe and placed two lids on each side of the pipe. I drilled a hole through one of the lids to make the pipe fit through, and drilled a hole through the other to make it fit on the motor. <div> </div> <div class="col-sm-4"> <img alt="INTRO" src="./ezgif-4-8cbc961c87.gif" class=center> </div> Although the force was not as strong as I wanted it to be, I still felt satisfied that this idea works not only in theory, but also in an actual creation that I can make.
